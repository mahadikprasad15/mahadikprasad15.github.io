Research Interests

My current work studies whether internal activations of language models contain behavior-relevant structure that can be used to build more transferable safety monitors. I am especially interested in linear probes, persona-conditioned representations, out-of-distribution generalization, deception, sycophancy, and evaluation of monitoring methods under distribution shift.

Current Work

Persona Axes Improve Cross-Dataset Generalization of Linear Probes

This project investigates whether persona-conditioned activation directions provide a useful inductive bias for detecting harmful behaviors such as deception and sycophancy. The work compares persona-derived principal components against raw activation probes across multiple datasets and model settings.

Manuscript in preparation for workshop submission, 2026.
